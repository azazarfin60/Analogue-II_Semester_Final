# 02: Amplifier Topologies

## All About Electronics
All About Electronics provides excellent visual walkthroughs and solved problems for various multistage and high-performance amplifier configurations:
- **Cascaded Multistage Amplifiers**: The conceptual working of cascaded amplifiers (BJT, FET) and loading effects of stages are covered in "BJT- Multistage Amplifier Explained (with Example)" starting at [0:16](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PoPBaoS-qQU&t=16s).
- **Darlington Pair Configuration**:
  - **Darlington as a Switch**: Derivation of the overall current gain ($\beta_D \approx \beta_1 \beta_2$) and switching characteristics are analyzed in "The Darlington Pair as a Switch" starting at [0:16](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=uQxMnBqNvyI&t=16s) (with the $\beta_D$ proof beginning at [2:33](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=uQxMnBqNvyI&t=153s)).
  - **Darlington as an Amplifier**: AC small-signal modeling, high input impedance ($Z_i \approx \beta_D R_E$), low output impedance ($Z_o$), and near-unity voltage gain ($A_v \approx 1$) are derived in "Darlington Pair as an Amplifier" starting at [0:17](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gXPipxBfIc4&t=17s) (with small-signal AC model at [4:27](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gXPipxBfIc4&t=267s)).
- **Cascode Amplifier Configuration**: MOSFET-based cascode amplifier configuration (Common Source input followed by Common Gate output stage) and active loads are analyzed in "Cascode Amplifier using MOSFET Explained" starting at [0:11](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=uFxXgDCLhGw&t=11s).

## Ankit Goyal Sir
Ankit Goyal Sir provides rigorous mathematical derivations and exam-oriented problem-solving for advanced multistage networks:
- **General Multistage Amplifiers**: Direct-coupled, RC-coupled, and transformer-coupled multi-transistor networks are covered in "Multi-stage Amplifiers | Part-1" starting at [03:12](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=D6T44XH92NU&t=192s).
- **Cascode Amplifier (BJT)**: Detailed small-signal AC model analysis of a cascaded Common-Emitter and Common-Base stage, proving high output resistance, high bandwidth (due to Miller effect elimination), and high voltage gain in "Multi-stage Amplifiers | Part-2" starting at [01:40](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mGgygCh-mmc&t=100s).
- **Darlington Pair (BJT)**: Extensive network analysis, biasing equations, base-to-emitter offset considerations ($2 \times V_{BE} = 1.4\text{ V}$), and overall current gain proofs are covered in "Multi-stage Amplifiers | Part-2" starting at [43:06](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mGgygCh-mmc&t=2586s).
